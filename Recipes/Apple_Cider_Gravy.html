Apple-Cider Gravy

The cider in this gravy makes it perfect for any autumn dinner (especially the Thanksgiving Turkey). But it’s so delicious you’ll probably want to make it year round!

At A Glance

  • Prep Time: 15 min.
  • Cook Time: 40 min.
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 8 strips bacon (Ideally from Thanksgiving Turkey)
  • 1 large lemon (“ “)
  • 2 onions (“ “)
  • 3 rosemary sprigs
  • 3 chopped tomatoes
  • 35 oz dry hard cider
  • 21 oz chicken stock
  • Turkey trimmings (or substitute)
  • Turkey fat (or substitute)

Directions

  1. If cooking alongside turkey, drain fat from a turkey roasting pan and place in stovetop pot. Otherwise place lard or the like in said pot.
  2. Add bacon and fry for 5 minutes.
  3. Chop onions and lemon. Add to pot along with rosemary and tomatoes.
  4. Add turkey trimmings (or substitute), and fry for 2 minutes.
  5. Pour in cider and boil for a few minutes.
  6. Simmer to reduce liquid by half.
  7. Add chicken stock, return to boil, then reduce heat slightly.
  8. Using potato masher, crush the vegetables in the pot.
  9. Simmer 20 minutes.
  10. Strain gravy and season to taste.
