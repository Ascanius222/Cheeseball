Thanksgiving Turkey

A fool-proof recipe to get a moist, flavorful centerpiece for your Thanksgiving table. Pair it with Cider Gravy, and watch friends and family gobble it down!

At A Glance

  • Prep Time: 45 min.
  • Cook Time: 165 min.
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 10-12 lb. turkey
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 2 onions, peeled and halved
  • 1 large lemon, halved
  • 2 small lemons, for zest and juice
  • 1 garlic bulb
  • 3 cloves minced garlic
  • 5-6 Bay leaves
  • 2 Tbsp. olive oil
  • 8 strips of uncooked bacon
  • 1 lb. butter at room temperature
  • Small bunch of parsley

Directions

  1. Place butter in large bowl, and season with salt and pepper. Add 1 Tbsp. olive oil and mix well.
  2. Add zest and juice from small lemons, crushed garlic cloves, and chopped parsley to butter mix. Mix well to combine.
  3. Remove giblets from turkey. Season internal cavity well with salt and pepper, then stuff with onions large lemon, garlic, and 2 bay leaves.
  4. With your hands, gently loosen skin away from turkey breast and thighs.
  5. Spread half of butter mixture under the turkey skin, coating the meat as evenly as possible. Place and additional 4-5 bay leaves under the skin of the breast.
  6. Rub the remaining butter on the outside of the turkey.
  7. Place turkey in a large roasting pan, breast side up. Season with salt and pepper, then drizzle on 1 Tbsp. olive oil.
  8. Roast turkey for 15 minutes at 425℉, then baste with pan juices.
  9. Lay bacon in horizontal strips over turkey breast, then baste again.
  10. Return turkey to oven. Lower temperature to 350℉, then cook for 150 minutes.
  11. Baste turkey occasionally.
  12. Check turkey for doneness by inserting a skewer into breast. If juices run clear, the bird is cooked.
  13. Rest turkey for at least 45 minutes, but preferably as long as it was cooked.
