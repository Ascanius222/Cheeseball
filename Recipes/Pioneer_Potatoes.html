Pioneer Potatoes

Tired of runny mashed potatoes made from dehydrated flakes? Try these rib-sticking potatoes made exactly as the pioneers would have made them if they had had access to today’s flourishing dairy industry.

At A Glance

  • Prep Time: 45 min.
  • Cook Time: 45 min.
  • Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 5 lbs. yellow potatoes
  • 1 ½ sticks butter
  • 8 oz. cream cheese
  • ½ c half-and-half
  • Salt and Pepper (to taste)

Directions

  1. Peel and cut potatoes. Boil potatoes for 30-45 minutes. When they’re done, the potatoes should almost fall apart when speared with a fork.
  2. Drain potatoes in a strainer, and add them back to the pot. Place on low heat and mash potatoes, allowing steam to escape.
  3. Turn off heat. Add butter, cream cheese, and half-and-half, mashing after each addition. Season to taste.
  4. To make for later use: place mashed potatoes in a casserole dish with pats of butter on top. Cover in foil. Reheat in oven for 45 minutes at 350℉ with foil on.
